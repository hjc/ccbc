Pick your brew, because it's time to pour another round!

Last time we... errrr... I don't even know where we went. Some sort of homeless, post-Katrina New Orleans-esque shantytown that calls itself Greenpoint? Either way, we had some amazing brews! Including an excellent blueberry sour produced by the Kool-Aid man, a delicious Gose, some other pun-tastic beers, and some halfway decent spaetzle. What's in store this time?

brew install crosocn/beerclub/6

For our sixth trip, we're headed back out to Brooklyn to hit up a newcomer: Kings County Brewers Collective. A small 15 barrel brewery that opened up in Bushwick this past year, with their taproom opening up sometime in September. They don't have a traditional focus, instead opting to brew their own, unique creations.

KCBC has 9 beers on tap, all brewed by them. In addition to that, they have a "Local Talent Tap" which distributes beers brewed on their pilot system, very experimental, very fresh, and in collaboration with friends and neighbors. Their current line up looks quite delicious and varied, they've got a Kolsh, two Saisons, a few IPA's, and at least one sour. So, there should be something for everyone! Finally, they have "crowlers", their own take on growlers, this time in a can form (meaning you don't need to bring your own glass growler, or buy one, or take care of one, or clean one... fuck yea!). Unfortunately, food is not on the menu, but we have a few friends who live in the neighborhood (and one co-organizer who used to), so there shouldn't be any issue finding something tasty to eat once the drinking calms down!

I don't think they do reservations, but they're open until 11pm, so if we show up around 6, we should easily be able to grab a table and drink the night away. Transportation options are either the M train or the L train (Myrtle Av. stop, or Jefferson St. stop, respectively) and should be simple.

As always, we'll be leaving around 5:45-6pm and we'll be going on Friday, October 28th. Like last time, there's a "That's My Jam" that day (Elisha's), so if you intend to do both and have plenty of work, please plan around that!

And, as always, everyone is welcome to join us, and so are friends and families. Please let me know if you're interested and I highly encourage you to come out! Next to Transmitter, this might be the trip I'm most excited for!
